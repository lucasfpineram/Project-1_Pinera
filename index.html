Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) is a martial art focused on grappling and ground fighting, emphasizing technique and leverage over brute strength. It encourages confidence, discipline, and fitness.

Benefits of practicing BJJ include stress relief, physical health, improved reflexes, and a welcoming community spirit.

My favourite martial artist is André Galvão.

History of BJJ

BJJ originated from Japanese Judo and Jujutsu brought to Brazil in the early 1900s by Mitsuyo Maeda. The Gracie family transformed it into an art centered on technique, timing, and leverage.

Today, BJJ is a global sport that helps individuals build resilience, learn self-defense, and grow physically and mentally.

Belt Ranks

Belt promotions reflect a student's progress and understanding. Stripes mark milestones on each belt before leveling up.

  • White to Blue: 1–2 years. Fundamentals and defense.
  • Blue to Purple: 2 years. Advanced techniques and pressure.
  • Purple to Brown: 1.5–2 years. Tactical and strategic refinement.
  • Brown to Black: 1–2 years. Full conceptual understanding.

How BJJ Points Work

Competitions reward positional control and progression. These maneuvers score points under IBJJF rules:

Takedown — 2 points. Taking the opponent to the ground with control.

Sweep — 2 points. Reversing from bottom to top position.

Knee on Belly — 2 points. Controlling the opponent with one knee on torso.

Guard Pass — 3 points. Advancing past the opponent's legs to a dominant position.

Mount — 4 points. Sitting on the chest with control.

Back Control — 4 points. Gaining control from behind with hooks in.
